Düşünce makinesi, öncelikle ortamda bulunan nesnelerin üzerine yansıyan ışığı algılar. Işık, nesnelerin yüzeyleriyle etkileşime girerek atomlar tarafından yansıtılır.     First, the think machine detects the light reflected on the objects in the environment. Light is reflected by atoms by interacting with the surfaces of objects. The think machine reads these reflections using the optical trace reading method. Optical trace reading analyzes the frequency, intensity, and other properties of the reflected light to obtain atom traces.

    The obtained atom traces are evaluated in the mathematical operations section. The think machine creates a backward design from these traces using complex mathematical algorithms. Mathematical operations can benefit from statistics, data analysis, modeling, and other related fields. These operations allow atom traces to be meaningfully processed and correlated with each other.

    Finally, after creating the backward design, the think machine presents or communicates the design to the user appropriately. The backward design may include information about the current situation by analyzing the past states and reflections of objects. This can be a potential tool for various applications that contribute to creative thinking in design areas or require backward analysis.
